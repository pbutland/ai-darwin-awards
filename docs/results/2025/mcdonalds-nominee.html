McDonald's AI Chatbot

Full scoring breakdown and rationale

Folly 75
Protecting a database of 64 million personal records with the password '123456' is a legendary level of incompetence that defies belief.
Arrogance 45
Deploying an AI to handle millions of job applications without implementing even the most basic security protocols is a case of overconfident amateurism.
Impact 60
This became a national incident, a major media story that highlighted the severe risks of entrusting sensitive data to poorly secured third-party AI systems.
Lethality 15
The exposure of personal data for 64 million people creates significant indirect risk of fraud, identity theft, and targeted scams.

Final Score 47.25
McDonald's and Paradox.ai achieved a spectacular fusion of cutting-edge AI and Stone Age security. They built a sophisticated chatbot to handle hiring, then protected the personal data of 64 million applicants with a password that would be too simple for a child's treehouse. The fact that the AI was already notorious for its inability to understand applicants adds a layer of delicious incompetence to the whole affair. This incident is a powerful reminder that the most advanced AI system is only as strong as its dumbest password.
